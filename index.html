Show Prompt: Test 0, Simple Anatomy Poster ▲ 
Create a square-format human anatomy educational diagram that prioritizes anatomical accuracy,
information density, and efficient use of space.

The image must be a multi-panel medical diagram filling at least 85 percent of the square
canvas, with minimal empty margins and no decorative borders.

At the top center, include the title "HUMAN ANATOMY" in clear, high-contrast lettering that is
readable at small sizes.

At the center of the image, place a front-facing adult male human figure with skin removed to
show the muscular system.

The figure should stand upright with arms relaxed at the sides and feet together.

Muscles must be anatomically correct, clearly separated, and accurately proportioned.

The central figure should be the largest and most visually dominant element.

Surrounding the central figure, arrange compact, clearly separated panels showing the
following systems, all fully visible and not cropped:

• Brain, shown separately with major regions identifiable
• Skeletal system, full front-facing skeleton
• Heart, standalone with major vessels
• Digestive system, from esophagus to intestines
• Eye, cross-sectional view with internal structures
• Cardiovascular system, heart with arteries and veins
• Urinary system, kidneys, ureters, bladder, urethra
• Secondary muscular detail panel highlighting named muscle groups

Each panel must include legible labels with thin leader lines pointing precisely to anatomical
structures.

Labels must use correct anatomical terminology with no misspellings and no invented anatomy.

The visual style should be clear medical textbook illustration, flat lighting, neutral
background, and high clarity.

Do not emphasize paper texture, aging, folds, stains, decorative borders, or faux historical
elements.

The background must remain visually quiet and must not compete with the anatomy.

The goal is a reference-quality anatomy chart, optimized for clarity, accuracy, and efficient
use of square space.
